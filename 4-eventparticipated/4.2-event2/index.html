Event 2

Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day 2026”

Event Information

  • Event Name: FCAJ Community Day 2026
  • Date & Time: Saturday, May 23, 2026, 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM
  • Location: Bitexco Financial Tower, Ho Chi Minh City
  • Role: Attendee

Event Objectives

  • Provide participants with practical knowledge about Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing, and modern software development.
  • Share technical insights from AWS experts and industry professionals.
  • Help attendees understand how AI, Amazon CloudFront, enterprise AI systems, and real-world product development can be applied in practice.
  • Create opportunities for students and technology learners to gain career-oriented knowledge from real industry experiences.

Key Topics

Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You

Speaker: Anh Tinh Truong

The opening session emphasized that the quality of AI-generated responses depends heavily on the context provided by users. Instead of relying only on prompts, modern AI systems increasingly combine memory and contextual information to deliver more accurate and personalized results.

Key ideas from this session included:

  • Understanding why AI may produce inaccurate responses when context is insufficient.
  • Learning the evolution from simple prompting to AI systems with memory, such as the Second AI Brain concept.
  • Applying practical techniques to provide richer context and improve AI performance.
  • Receiving career advice on how students can begin building AI-powered applications.

Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick

Speaker: Anh Pham

This session introduced Amazon Quick, a platform that enables organizations to create AI-powered assistants capable of analyzing data and supporting business decision-making.

Main features presented included:

  • Quick Chat Agent for exploring business data and generating insights.
  • Quick Flows for creating AI workflows using natural language without writing code.
  • Quick Spaces for knowledge sharing and team collaboration.
  • QuickSight integration for automatically generating dashboards and reports from raw datasets.

The presentation demonstrated how generative AI can simplify data analysis and improve productivity across organizations.

From Edge to Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

Speaker: Anh Thinh Nguyen

The speaker explained how Amazon CloudFront improves application performance by delivering content through AWS edge locations around the world.

Topics covered included:

  • Deploying CloudFront for different application workloads.
  • Improving website performance through caching and content delivery optimization.
  • Using security features such as HTTPS support, AWS WAF integration, and access control.
  • Applying cost optimization strategies when using CloudFront.
  • Increasing application availability and reliability with a global content delivery network.

This session highlighted CloudFront as an essential service for building scalable and high-performance web applications.

36 Hours with LotusHacks - Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality

Speakers: Chi Thao Nguyen, Chi Mai Nguyen, and Chi Uyen Le

Chi Thao Nguyen, Chi Mai Nguyen, and Chi Uyen Le from Team VIB shared their experience participating in the LotusHacks hackathon and developing the UTMorpho project within only 36 hours.

The presentation covered:

  • Brainstorming and selecting a suitable project idea.
  • Identifying real-world problems and defining project requirements.
  • Collaborating under tight deadlines during rapid software development.
  • Handling challenges encountered throughout the competition.
  • Demonstrating the completed product and discussing future improvements.

The session emphasized teamwork, time management, and iterative development under real project constraints.

Non-Determinism of Deterministic LLM Settings

Speaker: Anh Duc Dao

This technical presentation focused on how Large Language Models generate responses and explained why setting the temperature parameter to zero does not always guarantee identical outputs.

Important concepts included:

  • The token prediction process inside LLMs.
  • Why deterministic settings may still produce different responses.
  • The impact of inference optimization on output consistency.
  • Practical methods for reducing response variation in production AI systems.

Participants gained a better understanding of the limitations of LLM inference and strategies for building more reliable AI applications.

Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring

Speaker: Chi Vy Lam

The final session introduced the architecture of enterprise-level Multi-Agent AI systems through a startup credit scoring application.

Main topics included:

  • Challenges in evaluating startup creditworthiness using traditional banking systems.
  • Differences between Single-Agent and Multi-Agent architectures.
  • Designing a Virtual Credit Committee using multiple specialized AI agents.
  • Implementing guardrails, compliance mechanisms, and governance for enterprise AI.
  • Creating a deployment roadmap and identifying the expected business value of Multi-Agent systems.

The presentation demonstrated how multiple AI agents can collaborate to solve complex business problems more effectively than a single AI model.

What I Learned

AI Usage And Context Engineering

  • Providing clear and sufficient context is essential when interacting with AI systems.
  • AI assistants become more useful when they can combine prompts, memory, and relevant background information.
  • Context engineering is an important skill for students who want to build practical AI-powered applications.

AWS Services And Cloud Architecture

  • Amazon Quick shows how generative AI can support data exploration, workflow automation, and business intelligence.
  • Amazon CloudFront plays an important role in performance optimization, security, availability, and cost control for web applications.
  • AWS services can be combined to create reliable and scalable solutions for modern software systems.

Software Development And Enterprise AI

  • Hackathon projects provide valuable lessons in teamwork, rapid prototyping, and agile development under time constraints.
  • LLM outputs may vary even under deterministic configurations, so production AI systems need consistency control strategies.
  • Enterprise Multi-Agent systems can support complex decision-making scenarios, such as startup credit scoring, when designed with guardrails and governance.

Application

  • Use better context when working with AI tools to improve the quality of generated answers and technical support.
  • Apply CloudFront knowledge when building or deploying web applications that require fast and secure content delivery.
  • Practice rapid prototyping and teamwork skills learned from the hackathon experience.
  • Consider reliability, governance, and compliance when designing AI systems for real business use cases.
  • Continue learning AWS AI and cloud services to build stronger foundations for future projects.

Event Experience

Attending “FCAJ Community Day 2026” was a valuable experience because it combined practical AI knowledge, AWS cloud services, and real project stories from industry professionals. The sessions helped me understand not only technical concepts, but also how these technologies are applied in real products and business scenarios.

Learning from experienced speakers

  • The speakers shared real-world experience in AI, CloudFront, hackathon product development, LLM behavior, and enterprise AI systems.
  • Each topic provided a different perspective, from using AI effectively to deploying reliable cloud applications and designing multi-agent architectures.

Practical technical exposure

  • I gained a clearer understanding of how context affects AI responses and how to improve interactions with AI assistants.
  • I learned how Amazon CloudFront supports performance, security, and availability for modern web applications.
  • I also learned why LLM systems require careful testing and design even when using deterministic settings.

Lessons learned

  • AI tools are powerful, but their effectiveness depends on how well users provide context and define goals.
  • Cloud services such as CloudFront are important foundations for scalable application delivery.
  • Building real products requires teamwork, time management, iteration, and the ability to solve unexpected problems.
  • Enterprise AI systems must be designed with governance, compliance, and clear responsibilities.

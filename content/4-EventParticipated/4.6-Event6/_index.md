# Summary Report: "Swinburne Cloud Mastery 2026"

### Event Information

- **Event Name:** Swinburne Cloud Mastery 2026
- **Date & Time:** Saturday, July 4, 2026
- **Location:** Bitexco Financial Tower, Ho Chi Minh City
- **Role:** Attendee

### Event Objectives

Swinburne Cloud Mastery 2026 was organized to give students the opportunity to approach new trends in Cloud Computing and AWS through sharing sessions from professionals working in the industry. Besides technical updates, the event also helped students understand the job market, career direction, and the gap between university knowledge and real business requirements.

The event also created a place for people interested in technology to connect, exchange experience, and expand their network with experts and other learners who share the same interest in Cloud Computing.

### Key Topics

#### Connecting University and Industry

**Speaker:** Anh Duy Nguyen - Industry Liaison Officer, Swinburne Vietnam

The opening session focused on the gap between academic learning and the working environment. A common problem for students is that classroom knowledge alone may not fully prepare them for real projects, company workflows, and professional expectations.

The speaker emphasized the role of connecting universities with businesses. Besides studying technical subjects, students should actively join community programs, competitions, and real projects to build experience before graduation.

The practical value of this topic is that students can prepare earlier for professional work. Through real activities, learners can understand workplace processes, develop soft skills, and become more confident when entering the technology industry.

#### Career Opportunities in Cloud Computing

**Speaker:** Anh Nguyen Gia Hung - Head of Solutions Architecture

This session discussed the growth of Cloud Computing and the demand for cloud-related roles in the coming years. Many students know AWS is important, but they may not clearly understand which career paths are available or what skills they should prepare.

The speaker introduced common roles such as Cloud Engineer, Solutions Architect, and DevOps Engineer. He also highlighted foundational skills, continuous learning, problem-solving ability, and a proactive mindset when approaching new technologies.

The value of this session was career orientation. It helped me see that cloud careers require both technical knowledge and the ability to keep learning as services, tools, and business needs change.

#### Journey from Student to Data Engineer

**Speaker:** Anh Cam Vinh Binh - Data Engineer

This sharing brought a practical view of career development from being a student to becoming a Data Engineer. The challenge discussed was that students often need more than classroom knowledge to stand out when applying for technical roles.

The speaker shared experience in learning, building personal projects, and joining community activities to improve skills. The session also introduced some Data Architecture problems in enterprises and how data is used to support decision-making.

The practical value of this topic was understanding the importance of project experience. A strong foundation combined with hands-on practice can become a major advantage when applying for technical positions.

#### Fireside Chat - "How Can We Foster a Vietnamese Steve Jobs?"

**Speakers:** Chi Nhu Tran and Chi Nguyen Ngoc Quynh Mai

This discussion focused on innovation, entrepreneurship, and the role of education in developing high-quality technology talent. One key problem is that students may focus only on technical knowledge while paying less attention to creativity, adaptability, and self-development.

The speakers discussed the conditions needed to nurture creative thinking, innovation, and the courage to try challenging ideas. The session encouraged learners to develop themselves actively instead of limiting their growth to formal coursework.

The practical value of this discussion was mindset development. It reminded me that technology careers are not only about tools and services, but also about creativity, initiative, and the ability to adapt to change.

#### Bridging the Gap Between Learning and Real Work

**Speaker:** Anh Nguyen Khang - Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics

The final session focused on helping students understand the gap between academic training and real business requirements. A common issue is that students may have theoretical knowledge but still lack portfolio projects, hands-on experience, and practical problem-solving skills.

The speaker emphasized that students should join real projects, build a portfolio, practice with cloud services, and continuously improve their ability to solve problems. These elements can help candidates create stronger advantages during recruitment.

The practical value of this topic was very clear for career preparation. It showed me that learning AWS should be connected with real implementation, personal projects, and evidence of practical skills.

### What I Learned

- I understood more clearly the development trends of Cloud Computing and the demand for AWS-related roles.
- I gained a better direction for career paths such as Cloud Engineer, DevOps Engineer, and Solutions Architect.
- I realized the importance of joining real projects, building a portfolio, and gaining experience alongside theoretical learning.
- I learned more about the role of Data Engineering and Data Architecture in business digital transformation.
- I gained more perspective on innovation, continuous learning, and adaptability in the technology field.
- I understood that joining technology communities such as FCAJ and the AWS Community is a good way to expand knowledge, connect with experts, and develop future career opportunities.

### Application

The sharing sessions from this event gave me more direction while working on my **AWS Serverless Event Portal** project. The career and cloud architecture topics helped me better understand the importance of building backend services, databases, and AWS architecture in a structured way.

The advice about learning through real projects also motivated me to continue improving my skills through hands-on practice. For the Event Portal, this means paying attention not only to whether the features work, but also to how the backend, data model, documentation, and deployment approach can become clearer and more professional.

The Data Engineering and career preparation topics also reminded me that a project can become part of a portfolio if it is documented well and reflects practical problem-solving. I did not add new data engineering features from this event, but the session helped me think more seriously about how to present and improve my project experience.

### Event Experience

What impressed me most was the active learning atmosphere and the openness of the speakers. Each session brought a different perspective, from cloud career opportunities to data engineering, innovation, and the gap between school learning and real work.

Besides technical knowledge, the event also gave me a chance to connect with other students who are interested in AWS and Cloud Computing. This made the event feel motivating because I could see that many people are learning and preparing for similar career paths.

After attending the program, I felt more motivated to continue learning AWS, practicing with real services, and preparing better for my future career. The event reminded me that cloud learning should combine technical practice, community participation, and long-term self-development.
